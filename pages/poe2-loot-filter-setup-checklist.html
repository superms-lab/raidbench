POE2 loot filter setup checklist

A beginner-safe POE2 loot filter checklist for reducing screen clutter without hiding progression items too early.

View audit credits Back to POE2 Lab

Short answer

A loot filter should match your stage of progression. Early players need readability without hiding useful bases, currency, and upgrades; later players can tighten the filter around their build and farming route.

StageFilter goalAvoid
CampaignKeep upgrades, currency, and useful bases visible.Using an endgame-strict filter too early.
Early endgameHighlight build-relevant bases and currency.Hiding items before you understand their value.
Farming routeReduce noise around the content you repeat.Copying a filter made for a different economy or build.
ReviewUpdate after patch or build changes.Assuming one filter fits the whole season.

Checklist

  • Start less strict if you are still learning item value.
  • Keep build-relevant weapon and armor bases visible.
  • Review filter settings after changing builds.
  • Do not copy a high-end farming filter for campaign progression.
  • Use a manual review list for items you are unsure about.

Example

A new player may think a stricter filter is better, but hiding too much early can remove learning signals and slow upgrades.

Common mistakes

  • Using a streamer filter without understanding its assumptions.
  • Hiding progression bases too early.
  • Never updating the filter after changing builds.
  • Treating the filter as an economy guide.
