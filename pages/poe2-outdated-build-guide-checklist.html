POE2 outdated build guide checklist

A quick checklist for deciding whether a Path of Exile 2 build guide is still safe to follow after patches and economy shifts.

View audit credits Back to POE2 Lab

Short answer

Treat every build guide as version-sensitive until it proves otherwise. A good POE2 guide names the patch context, explains leveling separately from endgame, lists required items, and gives budget fallbacks when trade prices move.

CheckGood signalWarning sign
Patch contextNames the current update or recent balance period.No date, no patch, or comments say it stopped working.
Leveling pathSeparates campaign skills from endgame setup.Only shows final gear and assumes you already have it.
DefensesExplains life, energy shield, resistances, recovery, and mobility.Only talks about damage numbers.
Required itemsMarks uniques, weapon type, gems, and substitutions.One expensive item quietly carries the whole build.
Boss planShows where damage windows and movement matter.Only clears easy packs in a highlight clip.

Checklist

  • Look for the last updated date before copying passive, gem, or gear choices.
  • Check whether the guide still matches the current class, skill, and endgame systems.
  • Write down the required items before committing to the build.
  • Confirm there is a cheap version that still works before expensive upgrades.
  • Avoid builds that hide defenses behind perfect gear.

Example

If a build says it is a league starter but needs a rare weapon, multiple specific affixes, and a unique jewel before the damage works, treat it as an endgame build with a leveling problem.

Common mistakes

  • Copying a high-budget showcase as a starter build.
  • Ignoring patch comments under the guide.
  • Buying one expensive item before checking the rest of the gear path.
  • Following a build with no defensive explanation.
